Make any hat a propeller hat!

You will need 4 10mm round magnets, wire (ideally a jewelry header pin), and beads or spacers.

Add a pause on layer 29 of `propeller_bottom_base.stl`. Use pen to mark the north poles of each magnet, insert the magnets into the cavity, and continue printing. This will be your bottom piece that goes between your head and the hat.

Then, paint tree supports for only the center portion of `propeller_top_base_v2.stl`. Add a pause on layer 24. Insert the magnets and continue. These magnets won't be totally sealed inside; they're left exposed in order to make a stronger connection with the bottom base piece. Use hot glue to secure the magnets in place once your print is complete.

Use a jewelry header pin for the propeller wire. Add a bead spacer followed by the propeller, then trim and round off the header wire. You can optionally apply a heat gun to the propeller for 3-5 seconds and bend it to be more of a propeller shape (wear gloves to protect your hands from the heat).

Align the magnets in your top piece with the magnetic base. You're done!
